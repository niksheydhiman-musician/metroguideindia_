Complete Station List — Whitefield to MG Road (East to West)

The Whitefield (Kadugodi) to MG Road metro route on the Purple Line passes through 17 intermediate stops. The stations are grouped below by zone to help you identify your boarding or alighting point quickly.

  • S
    Whitefield (Kadugodi) — Start
    Eastern terminus of the Purple Line. Board from Platform 2 for all trains heading toward MG Road and Challaghatta. Since this is the terminal station, trains start empty here — guaranteed seating if you board at Kadugodi.
  • 1
    Hopefarm Channasandra
    First stop after Whitefield. Serves the Hopefarm Junction area — a key node for buses heading toward Marathahalli and Kundalahalli Gate.
  • 2
    Pattanduru Agrahara (ITPL)
    Serves the International Tech Park Bangalore (ITPL) — one of Whitefield's largest IT campuses. Major boarding point for tech professionals.
  • 3
    Sri Sathya Sai Hospital
    Named after the Sathya Sai Hospital on Whitefield Main Road. Also serves the Kadugodi residential belt.
  • 4
    Nallurhalli
    Residential and commercial belt between Whitefield and Kundalahalli. Popular with residents of Prestige Shantiniketan and nearby apartments.
  • 5
    Kundalahalli
    Serves the Kundalahalli Gate area, a major bus interchange point. BMTC buses connect to Marathahalli, Sarjapur Road, and Electronic City from here.
  • 6
    Seetharamapalya
    Primarily residential. Gateway to Mahadevapura and the Outer Ring Road tech belt.
  • 7
    Hoodi
    One of the busiest intermediate stations. Serves the Hoodi Junction area — a key residential node and feeder point for ORR-facing offices. Expect high boarding volumes.
  • 8
    Garudacharpalya
    Serves the Tin Factory junction area. Major BMTC interchange point connecting to Shivajinagar, Majestic, and KR Market.
  • 9
    Singayyanapalya
    Transitional stop between the ORR residential belt and KR Pura. Serves nearby residential layouts.
  • 10
    K.R. Pura (Krishnarajapuram) — Interchange
    Major connectivity node. Connects to KR Puram Railway Station (trains to Whitefield, Majestic, Yeshwanthpur). The upcoming Blue Line (Central Silk Board–KIAL) will also interchange here, eventually connecting to Kempegowda International Airport.
  • 11
    Benniganahalli
    Elevated station serving the Benniganahalli lake belt. Auto-rickshaw and cab stands available outside for last-mile access to Ramamurthy Nagar.
  • 12
    Baiyappanahalli
    Major station serving the Baiyappanahalli railway depot area. Connects to Baiyappanahalli Railway Station. Historically the eastern terminus of Phase 1 before the Whitefield extension opened.
  • 13
    Swami Vivekananda Road
    Elevated station at the edge of Old Madras Road. Gateway to the Banaswadi and Ramamurthy Nagar residential zones.
  • 14
    Indiranagar
    One of the most popular stations on the route. Serves Indiranagar — Bengaluru's trendiest neighbourhood for cafés, restaurants, pubs, and shopping. Underground station.
  • 15
    Halasuru (Ulsoor)
    Serves Ulsoor Lake and surrounding areas. Good access to Shivajinagar and Ulsoor Road. Underground station.
  • 16
    Trinity
    Underground station close to Trinity Circle and Residency Road. Corporate offices, hospitals, and the Defence Colony area are accessible from here.
  • END
    MG Road — Destination
    Underground station in the heart of Bengaluru's commercial district. Future Pink Line interchange (under construction, expected 2026). Three exit points serving Church Street, Brigade Road, and the MG Road Boulevard. See exit guide below.
⭐ Guaranteed Seat Hack: Since Whitefield (Kadugodi) is the terminal station, every train starts empty here. If you board at Kadugodi, you are guaranteed a seat — a significant advantage over boarding at Hopefarm, ITPL, or Hoodi, where trains already fill up. Factor in 5–10 extra minutes for the journey to Whitefield during peak hours if you want this benefit.

All Ways to Buy Your Ticket

  • Token at Station Counter or TVM: Standard ₹70 fare. Queue at the counter or use the Ticket Vending Machine — accepts UPI, card, and cash.
  • Namma Metro Smart Card: Get 10% off every ride. Purchase at any station. Top up at station counters, TVMs, or via the official Namma Metro app. Best value for daily commuters.
  • QR Ticket via Namma Metro App: Download the Namma Metro app on Android or iOS, buy a QR ticket instantly, and scan at the AFC gate. No queuing.
  • WhatsApp QR Ticket: Message +91 97424 99911 on WhatsApp, follow the prompts to book and pay, and receive a QR code instantly. Works without a separate app. Learn how WhatsApp ticketing works on Namma Metro →
  • Paytm / PhonePe: Both apps support Namma Metro QR ticketing directly from their transit sections.
  • Daily Unlimited Pass (₹150): Worth it if you plan to take 3+ rides in a day — for example, going to MG Road, returning, and then heading out again in the evening.
💡 Peak Hour Loyalty Tip: The Namma Metro app sometimes offers additional loyalty points or promotional discounts for frequent travellers. Check the app's offers section — BMRCL has run promotional campaigns with Paytm and PhonePe that reduce the effective per-ride cost further.

Whitefield (Kadugodi) Station — Everything You Need to Know

Kadugodi metro station is the eastern terminus of Namma Metro's Purple Line, inaugurated on 26 March 2023. It sits adjacent to the Whitefield Railway Station — a dual transport node for both suburban rail and metro commuters from the IT corridor.

Platform Intelligence

  • Platform 2: All trains toward MG Road, Indiranagar, Majestic, and Challaghatta depart from Platform 2. This is the only active departing platform.
  • Platform 1: Arriving trains from MG Road end their journey on Platform 1. Do not attempt to board from here.
  • Doors open on the left side of the train as you face the direction of travel (toward MG Road).

MG Road Metro Station — Arrival Guide

MG Road metro station is an underground station on the Purple Line — one of the five underground stations on the line, built using the cut-and-cover method. It sits beneath Mahatma Gandhi Road, the iconic commercial artery of central Bengaluru. The station is also a future Pink Line interchange point (under construction, expected to open in late 2026), which will make it one of the most significant nodes in the Namma Metro network.

Good news for early 2026 commuters: Kamaraj Road (the approach road at Gate 3) was fully reopened in January 2026 after nearly six years of metro construction work. Access to the station from that direction is now smoother than it has been in years.

Platform Details at MG Road

  • Platform 1: Toward Whitefield (Kadugodi) — board here for the return journey.
  • Platform 2: Toward Challaghatta — for onward travel to Majestic, Vijayanagar, and Kengeri.

The Exit Gate Strategy — Secret Sauce for MG Road

MG Road station has three exit points, and picking the wrong one can add a 10-minute walk to your destination. Here is exactly which gate to use:

Exit A
Church Street Side

Best for: Brigade Road, Church Street, Blossom Book House, Hard Rock Café, and Bengaluru's pub street. This exit puts you directly on Church Street — the fastest access to the Brigade Road commercial zone.

Exit B
MG Road Boulevard

Best for: The MG Road walking boulevard, Samsung Opera House, Metro Art Center, and the main MG Road pedestrian stretch. This is the most scenic exit — walk straight out onto the tree-lined boulevard.

Exit C
Kasturba Road / Cauvery Side

Best for: Cauvery Handicrafts Emporium, Kasturba Road, and offices along Museum Road. Also useful for reaching Cubbon Park on foot (approximately 10 minutes).

Golden Commuter Tips — What Other Guides Don't Tell You

🎯
Board at Kadugodi for a Guaranteed Seat

Whitefield (Kadugodi) is the terminus — every train starts empty here. If you board at Hopefarm, ITPL, or Hoodi, the train is already filling up. Those 5 extra minutes to reach Kadugodi can mean the difference between a comfortable seated commute and a standing crush.

⏱️
The Real-World Journey Time

Pure ride time is ~34 minutes. Add 30–45 seconds per stop (17 stations = ~12 minutes of dwell time built in). Add 5–8 minutes at Whitefield security during the 9 AM rush. Plan for 40–45 minutes door-to-platform during peak hours.

👩
Women's Coach Rule

The first coach of every Purple Line train has reserved seating for women at the front two doors heading toward MG Road. Male passengers should avoid these designated areas. This coach tends to be less crowded — female commuters can use this to their advantage at Whitefield.

📱
Skip the Queue — Go Digital

During peak hours at Whitefield, token counter queues can stretch to 5+ minutes. Use the Namma Metro app or WhatsApp chatbot (+91 97424 99911) to buy a QR ticket before you reach the station and walk straight to the AFC gates.

🔵
KR Pura — Future Airport Connection

At station 10 (K.R. Pura), the upcoming Blue Line will eventually connect to Kempegowda International Airport. Once operational, you'll be able to change here for the airport — a game-changer for Whitefield IT professionals who travel frequently.

🚕
MG Road Pickup Zone — Critical

Uber/Ola/Rapido pickups at MG Road station are only permitted at the designated zone near Exit A. Traffic police are active on MG Road and issue fines for illegal pickups on the main boulevard. Use the app to set the designated pickup point before you exit the station.

Last-Mile Connectivity

At Whitefield (Kadugodi)

  • BMTC Feeder Buses (MF prefix): MF-1, MF-2 routes connect the station to EPIP Zone, Varthur, and Hagadur. These run in sync with metro timings — look for the orange-and-white BMTC feeders at the bus bay outside Gate 1.
  • Auto-rickshaws: Prepaid auto counter available outside the station. Shared autos run toward ITPL and Marathahalli. Negotiate firmly for unmetered autos — always insist on the meter.
  • Ola/Uber/Rapido: Pickup zone on the approach road, Gate 1 side. Expect 3–5 minute arrival times. Surge pricing during 8:30–10 AM.
  • Whitefield Railway Station: 5-minute walk. Suburban trains connect to KSR Bengaluru (City Station), Yeshwanthpur, and Majestic via the railway route.

At MG Road

  • Designated cab pickup (Exit A): Use only the marked pickup zone near Exit A. The app will show this as the pin location for MG Road metro station.
  • Rapido Bike Taxi: Fastest for short hops to Commercial Street, Shivajinagar Bus Terminal, or Residency Road. Pickup from Exit A or B depending on destination.
  • Walking: From Exit A — Brigade Road (5 min), Church Street (2 min). From Exit B — MG Road Boulevard (immediate). From Exit C — Cubbon Park (10 min), Cauvery Emporium (3 min).
  • BMTC Buses: Multiple routes stop on MG Road at street level — including services to Shivajinagar, KR Market, and Banashankari.

MG Road to Whitefield Metro — The Return Journey

The MG Road to Whitefield metro return journey uses the same Purple Line — just in the opposite direction. Board from Platform 1 at MG Road station toward Whitefield (Kadugodi). The journey covers the same 19 stations in the same 34–38 minutes.

Trains on the return run from MG Road toward Whitefield from as early as 05:58 AM. The last train from MG Road toward Whitefield departs around 11:00 PM. Check the Namma Metro official website or the app for the most current schedule before a late-night return.

🌙 Late Night Tip: If you're heading back to Whitefield after a late evening on MG Road or Brigade Road, the last Purple Line train toward Kadugodi runs around 11:00 PM. Post that, your options are cabs (surge pricing likely) or the Whitefield Railway Station overnight services. Plan accordingly — missing the last metro from MG Road is genuinely inconvenient.

Accessibility & Safety

  • Wheelchair access: Both Whitefield (Kadugodi) and MG Road stations are 100% wheelchair and stroller accessible with dedicated lifts at all entry/exit points.
  • Visual impairments: Tactile flooring guides are present throughout both stations from gate to platform.
  • Emergency helpline: 1511 — available 24/7. Staff are stationed at both ends at all operating hours.
  • Women's safety: First coach reserved seating. CCTV coverage throughout all 37 Purple Line stations. BMRCL has women security officers deployed at major stations including Whitefield and MG Road.
  • Pickpocket alert: Both Whitefield (boarding) and MG Road (alighting) are high-traffic stations. Keep your phone and wallet in a front pocket or a closed bag, especially during peak-hour crowds.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many stops from Whitefield to MG Road?

There are 17 intermediate stations between Whitefield (Kadugodi) and MG Road on the Purple Line. The journey is direct — no interchange required.

What is the fare from Whitefield to MG Road metro?

The standard token fare is ₹70. With a Namma Metro Smart Card, you pay ₹63 (10% discount). QR tickets via the Namma Metro app or WhatsApp are also available at ₹70. The Daily Unlimited Pass at ₹150 is worth buying if you plan 3+ trips in a day.

What is the travel time from Whitefield to MG Road?

The journey takes approximately 34–38 minutes depending on peak/off-peak conditions. Dwell time at 17 intermediate stops accounts for about 12 minutes of this. Add 5–10 minutes for security and platform time at Whitefield during the morning rush.

Which platform do I board from at Whitefield (Kadugodi)?

Board from Platform 2 at Whitefield (Kadugodi) for all trains heading toward MG Road and Challaghatta. Platform 1 is for arriving trains — do not board from there.

Which exit at MG Road is best for Brigade Road?

Use Exit A (Church Street side) for Brigade Road, Church Street, and the pub/restaurant strip. Exit B is best for the MG Road Boulevard and Samsung Opera House. Exit C is for Kasturba Road and Cauvery Handicrafts.

Is there parking at Kadugodi metro station?

Yes — Whitefield (Kadugodi) has one of the largest parking facilities on the Purple Line. Rates are approximately ₹30 for two-wheelers and ₹60 for four-wheelers for the initial hours. Monthly passes are available for regular commuters. Arrive before 8:30 AM on weekdays for a guaranteed spot. MG Road has no parking — do not drive there.

Can I use WhatsApp to buy a ticket for this route?

Yes. Message +91 97424 99911 on WhatsApp, choose your language, select "Buy Ticket," enter your journey details, pay via UPI, and receive a QR code instantly. Scan it at the AFC gate at Whitefield. Full instructions are available on the Namma Metro WhatsApp ticketing page.
