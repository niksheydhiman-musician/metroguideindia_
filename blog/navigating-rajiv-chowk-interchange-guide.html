Navigating Rajiv Chowk Interchange: A Stress-Free Guide

📅 8 April 2026 ✍️ MetroGuideIndia ⏱️ 6 min read

Rajiv Chowk is one of the busiest Delhi Metro stations, connecting the Blue Line and Yellow Line under Connaught Place. The interchange can feel crowded, but with the right coach choice and walking direction, you can switch lines quickly and avoid confusion.

Which Lines Meet at Rajiv Chowk?

  • Blue Line: Dwarka Sector 21 ↔ Noida Electronic City / Vaishali
  • Yellow Line: Millennium City Centre Gurugram ↔ Samaypur Badli

If your destination is central Delhi, Rajiv Chowk is often the fastest interchange point because of direct line connectivity and frequent train headways.

Step-by-Step Interchange Flow

  • Get down and follow overhead signs marked Interchange.
  • Use central corridors and keep to the left during peak movement.
  • Follow platform direction boards carefully for train destination.
  • Reach the correct platform side before the train arrives to avoid cross-flow.
Pro-Tip: For faster transfer, stand near coach sections that align with staircase/escalator positions at your boarding station.

Best Timing to Avoid Peak Crowds

  • High rush: 8:30 AM–10:30 AM and 5:30 PM–8:30 PM
  • Relatively smoother: 11:30 AM–4:30 PM
  • Weekend afternoons: manageable but tourist-heavy near Connaught Place exits
Pro-Tip: If possible, plan a 10–15 minute buffer when changing lines during weekday rush hours.

Exit Strategy for Connaught Place

Rajiv Chowk has multiple exits opening into inner and outer circles of Connaught Place. Check your destination (Block, road, or landmark) before selecting an exit so you do not lose time walking above ground.

  • Use station signage maps before tapping out.
  • If unsure, ask station staff for nearest gate to your block.
  • For first-time visitors, keep your map app open after exiting.

Final Takeaway

Rajiv Chowk interchange is very manageable once you know line directions, crowd windows, and your exit gate. A little pre-planning makes your transfer quicker, calmer, and more predictable.
