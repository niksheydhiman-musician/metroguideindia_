Delhi-Meerut RRTS: Complete Guide to Stations, Parking, and Last-Mile Connectivity

The Namo Bharat RRTS (Rapid Rail Transit System) is India's fastest regional transit network, connecting Delhi to Meerut in under 60 minutes. With 15 operational stations across 82.15 km, understanding parking options and last-mile connectivity at each station is essential for a smooth commute. This guide covers everything — from Delhi to Meerut travel time to RRTS last-mile connectivity solutions at every major station.

Namo Bharat RRTS Stations: Complete List

The full Namo Bharat RRTS stations list covers 15 stations: Sarai Kale Khan, New Ashok Nagar, Anand Vihar, Sahibabad, Ghaziabad, Guldhar, Duhai, Murad Nagar, Modi Nagar South, Modi Nagar North, Meerut South, Shatabdi Nagar, Begumpul, and Modipuram. The Rapid Rail Meerut distance from the Delhi end (Sarai Kale Khan) to Modipuram is approximately 82 km.

Delhi to Meerut Travel Time by RRTS

The Delhi to Meerut travel time by Namo Bharat RRTS is approximately 55–60 minutes from Sarai Kale Khan (Delhi) to Modipuram (Meerut). The RRTS operates at speeds up to 180 km/h, making it the fastest surface transit in India. Compare this to 2–3 hours by road during peak traffic.

Meerut Metro parking is available at all four major RRTS-Meerut Metro interchange stations. These multi-level parking facilities are managed by NCRTC and offer secure, reasonably priced parking for commuters arriving by car or two-wheeler.

RRTS Last-Mile Connectivity Options

RRTS last-mile connectivity is a key focus of NCRTC's urban integration plan. Here are the options available at major stations:

  • Auto-rickshaws & E-rickshaws: Available at all stations. E-rickshaws provide affordable short-distance connectivity in Ghaziabad and Meerut areas.
  • City buses (UPSRTC): Bus stops are co-located at Sarai Kale Khan, Anand Vihar, Ghaziabad, and Modipuram.
  • Cab aggregators (Ola/Uber): Available at designated pick-up zones at Sarai Kale Khan, Anand Vihar, and Ghaziabad.
  • Delhi Metro interchange: Sarai Kale Khan (Pink Line) and Anand Vihar (Blue/Pink Line) offer seamless metro-to-RRTS transfers.
  • Meerut Metro interchange: At Meerut South, Shatabdi Nagar, Begumpul, and Modipuram — see our Meerut Metro Interchange Guide for details.
  • Cycling & NMT: Dedicated cycle tracks and non-motorised transport lanes connect RRTS stations to nearby localities.

Key Stations: Last-Mile Connectivity Details

Sarai Kale Khan (Delhi)

The Delhi terminus connects to Delhi Metro Pink Line, ISBT Sarai Kale Khan (inter-city buses), and multiple auto/cab stands. It is the primary gateway for South and Central Delhi commuters.

Anand Vihar (Delhi)

Anand Vihar is a major transport hub connecting RRTS, Delhi Metro Blue & Pink Lines, Anand Vihar Railway Terminal, and the Anand Vihar ISBT. Ola/Uber pickup zones are available outside Gate 2.

Ghaziabad

Ghaziabad station connects to the city centre via UPSRTC city buses and e-rickshaws. The station is a major hub for commuters from Raj Nagar, Kavi Nagar, and Indirapuram.

Modipuram (Meerut End)

Modipuram is the northern terminus of both the RRTS and Meerut Metro. Multi-level parking, e-rickshaw stands, and Meerut city bus connections are available. It serves as the primary RRTS gateway for North Meerut.

Rapid Rail Meerut Distance & Route Summary

The Rapid Rail Meerut distance — the full RRTS corridor — spans 82.15 km from Sarai Kale Khan (Delhi) to Modipuram (Meerut). The line crosses three states' equivalent zones and includes elevated, at-grade, and underground sections near Sarai Kale Khan.
